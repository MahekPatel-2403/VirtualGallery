Welcome to My Virtual Art Gallery

A curated collection of some of the most inspiring artworks in history.

The Starry Night

Painted by Vincent van Gogh in 1889, this is one of the most recognized paintings in the history of Western culture.
The painting's style is Post-Impressionism. It shows the view from his asylum room, but the village is imaginary.
Fun Fact: The flame-like, dark structure dominating the left side is a pair of Cyprus trees. A previous description called it a tower.

Mona Lisa

This masterpiece was created by the Italian artist Leonardo da Vinci. It is believed to have been painted between 1503 and 1506.
The subject's enigmatic smile is one of its most famous features. The style is High Renaissance. It is currently housed in the Louvre Museum, Paris.

The Persistence of Memory

A 1931 painting by artist Salvador Dalí, and one of the most recognizable works of Surrealism.
It is widely recognized for its striking image of soft, melting pocket watches. The landscape is based on the coast of Catalonia, Spain. It was initially disliked by critics but is now a celebrated masterpiece.

The Scream

This is a composition created by Norwegian artist Edvard Munch between 1893 and 1910.
The agonized face in the painting has become one of the most iconic images of art, seen as symbolizing the anxiety of the human condition. The style is classified as Expressionism.

Girl with a Pearl Earring

An oil painting by Dutch Golden Age painter Johannes Vermeer, dated c. 1665.
Going by various names over the centuries, it became known by its present title towards the end of the 20th century. The work is a tronie, a Dutch term for a head-and-shoulders depiction that was not intended to be a portrait.
